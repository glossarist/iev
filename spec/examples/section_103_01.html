The IEV is published by the IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission) and is the authoritative online source for electrotechnical terminology in multiple languages. For each concept code, Electropedia provides a detail page with the term in English, French, and other languages, the definition, and source references. The vocabulary covers mathematics, physics, electrical engineering, electronics, telecommunications, and related disciplines.

Section 103-01 falls within subject area 103 (Mathematics — Functions) of IEV 60050. The terms in this section establish the foundational vocabulary for functions, including function as a mathematical object, functionals that take functions as arguments, the concepts of argument and value, and the range of a function. They also cover more specialised notions such as injectivity, surjectivity, bijectivity, inverse functions, composition, and analytic properties of functions.

The mathematical foundations established in section 103-01 are referenced throughout the rest of IEV 60050. Functions and functionals appear in every chapter of the vocabulary: from the description of physical quantities and their relationships, to the formulation of electromagnetic theory in terms of vector and scalar fields, to the characterisation of signals and systems in telecommunications and signal processing.

In particular, the notion of a functional is central to variational principles in physics, where action functionals are minimised to derive equations of motion. The notion of a function as a mapping between sets underpins the formal description of transformations in linear algebra, group theory, and topology, all of which appear in the mathematical chapters of the IEV.

The distinction between domain, codomain, and range is essential for precise mathematical communication, especially when working with partial functions, multivalued functions, or distributions. The IEV provides the standardised terminology that enables unambiguous specification of these concepts across technical publications, standards documents, and engineering practice.
